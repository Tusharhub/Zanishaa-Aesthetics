Commonly Asked Questions

Laser hair removal works by emitting a concentrated beam of light that is absorbed by the melanin (pigment) in hair follicles. This damages the hair follicles and inhibits future hair growth, resulting in smooth, hair-free skin.

Yes, laser hair removal is generally safe when performed by a qualified professional. However, there may be some temporary side effects such as redness or irritation, which typically subside within a few days.

The number of sessions required for laser hair removal varies depending on factors such as the area being treated, hair color, and skin type. Generally, multiple sessions spaced several weeks apart are needed to achieve optimal results.

Laser hair removal may cause some discomfort, often described as a sensation similar to snapping a rubber band against the skin. However, most people find the discomfort to be tolerable, and the procedure is typically well-tolerated with the use of topical numbing creams or cooling techniques.

Laser hair removal can be performed on almost any area of the body, including the face, arms, legs, underarms, bikini area, and back. However, it's essential to consult with a qualified professional to determine the suitability of treatment for specific areas.

The results of laser hair removal are long-lasting, with many people experiencing permanent hair reduction. However, some individuals may require occasional maintenance treatments to target any regrowth that occurs over time.

Most people can resume their normal activities immediately after laser hair removal treatment. However, it's essential to avoid sun exposure and activities that may irritate the treated area, such as hot baths or strenuous exercise, for a few days following the procedure.
